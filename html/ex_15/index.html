            According to third-quarter figures from Manitoba Finance obtained by the Winnipeg Sun, the province is forecasting a core government deficit of $775 million for the
             current fiscal year, slightly below the projected budget shortfall of $779 million.

            million.
            
            The 2017-18 fiscal year ends March 31.
            
            The improvement to governments bottom line comes despite an estimated $262-million loss in provincial income-tax revenue this year triggered by changes to federal 
            tax rates in 2016. The Trudeau government increased the top federal tax rate for income above $200,000 to 33% from 29%. That caused a short-term surge in tax revenue
            for provinces as high-income earners reported more of their income before the new tax rate took effect. But it had the opposite effect in subsequent years as less
            high-income revenue was reported for legitimate tax-planning reasons.
